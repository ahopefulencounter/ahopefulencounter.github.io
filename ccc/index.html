About California Conservation Corps

The California Conservation Corps (CCC) is the oldest and largest conservation corps in the nation. Created in 1976, the CCC's mission is to protect and enhance California's natural resources and communities while empowering and developing young adults through hard work and education.

Our Programs

Through our partnership with A Hopeful Encounter, we offer specialized programs to introduce Southeast Asian youth to California's National Parks and conservation careers.

Trail Stewards Program

Build and maintain trails in National Parks while earning certifications in trail construction and maintenance.

Fire & Forestry Program

Learn fire prevention techniques and forest management in California's parklands.

Park Interpreters Program

Develop skills to educate visitors about park ecosystems and cultural history.

California's National Parks

Through our program, you'll have the opportunity to work in and explore some of California's most spectacular national parks:

Yosemite National Park

Work on trail maintenance and habitat restoration in this iconic park.

Sequoia & Kings Canyon

Help protect giant sequoias and their ecosystems.

Redwood National Park

Work with the tallest trees on earth in northern California.

Program Benefits

What you'll gain from participating in our CCC Youth Program

Earn While You Learn

Corpsmembers earn a monthly stipend of $2,814 while gaining valuable experience.

Professional Certifications

Earn certifications in first aid, chainsaw operation, wildland firefighting, and more.

Education Benefits

Up to $8,000 in education awards and scholarships upon completion.

Career Pathways

Connect with potential employers in conservation, parks, and natural resources.
